It is the intersection of [[State]] and [[Behavior]] in [[OOP]], i.e. the latter regulating the interface to access the former. Put differently, it is the [[State]] of the [[Object]] filtered through its [[Behavior]]. In Ruby they are meant to *simulate* direct access to the [[Object]]'s [[Instance variable]]s.

Ruby has a built-in way to automatically create [[Getter method]]s, [[Setter method]]s and [[Instance variable]]s (hence attributes). It's using [[Accessor method]]s.
