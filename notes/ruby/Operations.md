Addition, Subtraction and Multiplication are high level concepts that break down as  [[Method]] in Ruby,  their effect depend on the type of [[Object]] invoked upon. What these [[Method]] return can be expressed as "evaluates to", when speaking at higher level or a the [[Expression]] level.

* For [[Integer]] we have:[ `#%`, `#*`, `#**`, `#+`, `#-`, `#/` ](https://docs.ruby-lang.org/en/3.2/Integer.html#method-i-2A)
* For [[Float]]: [ `#%`, `#*`, `#**`, `#+`, `#-`, `#/` ](https://docs.ruby-lang.org/en/3.2/Float.html#method-i-2A)
* For [[String]]:  [`#*`,`#+`](https://docs.ruby-lang.org/en/3.2/String.html#method-i-2A) 
* For [[Array]]:   [`#*`, `#+`, `#-`](https://docs.ruby-lang.org/en/3.2/Array.html#method-i-2A)
